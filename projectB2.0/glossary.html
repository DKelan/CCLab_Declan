Bias Glossary

Youngest: Superlative adjective - indicating higher extremes evokes a more extreme message from the reader

Unrelenting: Adjective - with a negative connotation to mean doing something with arrogance

Tirelessly: Adverb - stresses the amount of effort put in by China to deliver these vaccines to children

Galvanized/Roll up a Sleeve: Speculation: used as a nudge against Communism

Galvanized - Verb - excite people into action

Roll up a Sleeve - Phrase - Seen on propaganda posters, especially in the Soviet Union

Language to Watch For:

ADJECTIVES/ADVERBS:Will explicitly show the author's opinion on a subject based on how they describe something/an action.

E.x. The outrageous policies instituted by country X triggered national outrage nation-wide

The criminal's parents purged all evidence of their son's involvement

VERBS: Much like adverbs, but harder to invoke the author's exact bias. Some verbs, such as galvanized, happen to have a connotation to them; these connotations can come from historical precedent, political messages, other news bias, pop culture, etc.

E.x. This country was decimated by natural disaster X, and are in the process of dealing with the disaster's reprecussions
